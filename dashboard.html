Malaria

Malaria
Signs and Symptoms

Fever (This is the most common symptom), Chills, Headache, Sweats, Fatigue, Nausea and vomiting, Body aches, Generally feeling sick.

MNT
Food Allowed Food Not Allowed
Fresh fruits and vegetables work wonders for malaria patients. Avoid intake of coffee, tea, cocoa, cola or any other caffeinated beverages.
According to studies, vitamin A and vitamin C rich fruits and vegetables like beetroot, carrot, papaya, sweet lime, grapes, berries, lemon, orange help to detoxify and boost the immunity of the patient suffering from malaria. Refrain from having food that is spicy and/or hot. It will result in unnecessary stomach problems and heartburn. Sauces and pickles shouldn't be included anywhere in the diet for a malaria patient.

Typhoid

Typhoid
Signs and Symptoms

Early symptoms include fever, general ill-feeling, and abdominal pain, High fever (103°F, or 39.5°C) or higher Bloody stools, Chills, Agitation, confusion, delirium, seeing or hearing things that are not there (hallucinations), Difficulty paying attention (attention deficit), Nosebleeds, Severe fatigue Slow, sluggish, weak feeling

MNT
Food Allowed Food Not Allowed
Cooked vegetables can be consumed during typhoid so that the body receives vital nutrients to get stronger. Well cooked or boiled vegetables like potatoes, carrots, beets, raw papaya and squash can be consumed. They are easy to digest and provide nutrients. Avoid all the food items that are high in fibre as they are difficult to digest and put pressure on your digestive system. During typhoid, patients have an upset stomach and eating fibre-rich food items worsens it. Avoid raw fruits, vegetables, oats, barley, seeds, whole grains, nuts, and legumes.
